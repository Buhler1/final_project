All About Climbing!

Top Roping
Top rope climbing is a style in climbing in which the climber is securely attached to a rope which then passes up, through an anchor system at the top of the climb, and down to a belayer at the foot of the climb.
Lead Climbing
When lead climbing the climber ascends the route, periodically placing protection for safety in the event of a fall. As lead climbing does not require a pre-placed anchor at the top of the route, it is often seen as less restricted than top roping.
Bouldering
Bouldering is a form of rock climbing that is performed without the use of ropes or harnesses. While it can be done without any equipment whatsoever, most climbers use climbing shoes to help secure footholds, chalk to keep their hands dry, and bouldering mats to prevent injuries from falls.
